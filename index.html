Security

Password

How to Change Your Password

To change your password, press Ctrl + Alt + Delete and select Change Password.

This is the only approved method for changing your password.

Important: Please only update your password when you are in the office (Frontiers) or connected to the VPN.

Password Best Practice

Your password should include:

  • Uppercase letters
  • Lowercase letters
  • Numbers
  • Special characters

Using a strong password helps protect your account and company systems.

💬
Presenter Notes

The good news is that, thanks to the MFA, we no longer have to deal with the annoying routine of changing passwords every 90 days. That said, you do still need to change your password now, for the obvious reason that everyone is currently using a temporary one. And since you'll likely be keeping your new password for a while, make it a strong one: use uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

And here's how to change your password: press Ctrl + Alt + Delete and select "Change a password." A web page will then pop up — that's completely normal and intentional. Just follow the prompts from there.

Know the Difference

Email & Login Guidelines

When to use .net vs .org

firstname.lastname@frontiersin.net

Internal Identity
  • Use for Microsoft applications (Teams, SharePoint, Outlook)
  • Required for SSO access to all internal systems
  • Your primary identity for internal applications and services

firstname.lastname@frontiersin.org

Communication & External
  • Use for email communications
  • Use to log in to external applications
  • Outside of internal network
Key Reminder: Always use .net for internal apps and .org for emails and external logins.
💬
Presenter Notes

Some of you may already have noticed that when we log in, we use a .net account, while our actual email addresses end in .org. I want to highlight this because it is a very common point of confusion.

Your .net account is used for internal login — this includes your laptop and internal Microsoft applications. Your .org account is used for email, as well as for logging in to external services or applications.

In short: use .net for internal access, and .org for email and external login.

Security

Encryption

BitLocker Drive Encryption

All Frontiers computers and laptops are encrypted using BitLocker.

BitLocker Drive Encryption is a security feature built into the operating system that helps protect data from unauthorized access.

It protects company information in case a computer is lost, stolen, or improperly decommissioned.

💬
Presenter Notes

All our machines are encrypted with BitLocker. That means if you accidentally leave your laptop on the subway, whoever picks it up gets a very expensive, very heavy paperweight — but not our company data. BitLocker is one of those quiet behind-the-scenes heroes, a bit like the IT Service team: it works in the background to keep your data safe without requiring any action from you.

Security

VPN

Cloudflare WARP

We use Cloudflare to protect and improve the performance of our websites and online services.

Cloudflare provides security and reliability by protecting our systems against cyber threats, filtering malicious traffic, and ensuring fast and stable access to our services.

This helps keep our platforms secure, available, and performing well for users worldwide.

💬
Presenter Notes

So, if BitLocker protects the data stored on your laptop, how do we protect data while it's being transmitted? Many of you have probably already guessed the answer: VPN.

Our VPN is Cloudflare WARP. It sits quietly in your taskbar and creates a secure tunnel for your connection. Please make sure it shows "Connected." If you switch it off for no particular reason, several of your daily tools will stop working, and there is a very good chance your next IT ticket will end with us politely telling you to turn your VPN back on.

File Sharing & Data Backup

OneDrive

We use OneDrive to automatically save a copy of your files in the cloud.

This means that if your computer is lost, stolen, or stops working, your files are still safe and can easily be restored on a new device.

📁 Documents 🖼️ Pictures 🖥️ Desktop

Tech Tip

Be sure to keep your laptop connected to the network and switched on to ensure a complete backup. Also, double-check the files are syncing from time to time.

💬
Presenter Notes

OneDrive is your personal backup, automatically saving files to the cloud. It explicitly syncs three folders: Documents, Pictures, and Desktop. So if your masterpiece is sitting safely in one of those, you're in good shape. If, however, you leave it in Downloads and your hard drive gives up, we will be sympathetic — but we will not be performing miracles.

If OneDrive is your personal filing cabinet, SharePoint is the company library. It's the place for official policies, forms, and team documents — in other words, anything that needs to be shared and properly stored, rather than living quietly on one person's laptop.

We use 1Password, so you can finally stop hiding passwords on sticky notes under your keyboard. If you haven't set it up yet, have a look in your inbox — you should have received an email about 1Password with instructions on how to install it and get everything configured.

When Things Go Wrong

Logging a Ticket

How to Submit an IT Request

Primary

Frontiers IT App

Submit your request via the Frontiers IT App on MS Teams

Secondary

IT Service Desk / Help Center

Use the IT Service Desk or Help Center portal

Alternative

Email

Email it.services@frontiersin.org — this automatically creates a ticket in our system.

Tech Tip

Logging a ticket is essential to ensure your request is properly tracked, assigned to a Technician, and resolved efficiently.

Note: If you are locked out and cannot access your Frontiers email, you may send the request from your personal email.
💬
Presenter Notes

If something breaks — and, trust me, at some point it will — we need to know about it. The best way to submit a request is through the Frontiers IT App in Microsoft Teams. Alternatively, you can email it.services@frontiersin.org.

Please make sure a ticket is always logged. This is mandatory. It helps us track issues properly, keep a record of what's happening, and make sure nothing gets lost or forgotten along the way.

If you really have no other option — for example, you can't access your laptop at all — you can email us from your personal address as well. But ideally, that should be a last resort, because it usually means extra back-and-forth to verify your identity, which takes more time.
